## Q&A Preparation

### Technical Questions

**Q: "How do you handle the feedback loops in real-time?"**
A: "We use an event-driven architecture where engagement events trigger async learning jobs. The system updates prompt weights and user profiles without blocking the main application flow."

**Q: "What's your approach to scaling the AI processing?"**
A: "We've designed for horizontal scaling with background job queues. Heavy AI tasks run asynchronously, and we cache frequently used models and embeddings."

**Q: "How do you ensure the AI doesn't drift or degrade?"**
A: "We track performance metrics continuously and have rollback mechanisms for prompt versions. The learning system includes confidence thresholds and human oversight capabilities."

### Business Questions

**Q: "What's your go-to-market strategy?"**
A: "We start with SMBs and content creators who need efficiency gains, then move upmarket to enterprises who want custom AI models trained on their data."

**Q: "How is this different from existing tools like Hootsuite or Buffer?"**
A: "Traditional tools are scheduling platforms. We're an intelligence platform. Our AI gets smarter with every interaction, while theirs stay static."

**Q: "What's your defensible moat?"**
A: "The cross-module learning creates network effects. The more customers use it, the better it gets for everyone. Plus, our proprietary feedback loops are hard to replicate."

### Investment Questions

**Q: "What's your revenue model?"**
A: "SaaS subscriptions starting at $49/month for individuals, $199/month for teams, and enterprise licensing for custom deployments."

**Q: "How big is the market?"**
A: "Content marketing software is $16B, video editing is $8B, and personalization engines are $12B. We're creating a new category that combines all three."

**Q: "What's your funding need and timeline?"**
A: "We need $2M seed to build out the team and integrate real AI APIs. 18-month runway to Series A with strong traction metrics."
